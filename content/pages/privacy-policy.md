## General Information

Based on Article 13 of the Swiss Federal Constitution and the provisions of the Swiss Federal Data Protection Act (DSG), every person has the right to privacy and protection against misuse of their personal data.

Renao takes the protection of personal information very seriously. All personal data is handled confidentially and in accordance with legal requirements and this privacy policy. In cooperation with our hosting providers, we strive to protect databases from unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or falsification.

Please note that data transmission over the Internet (for example via email) may have security gaps. Complete protection of data against access by third parties is not possible.

By using this website, you consent to the collection, processing, and use of data as described below. You may generally visit this website without registration. When you access pages, certain data such as visited pages, file names, date, and time may be stored on the server for statistical purposes without being directly linked to your identity. Personal data such as name, address, or email address is collected only when voluntarily provided. Without explicit consent, such data will not be shared with third parties.

## Processing of Personal Data

Personal data refers to all information relating to an identified or identifiable person. Processing includes any operation involving personal data such as storage, use, disclosure, modification, or deletion.

We process personal data in accordance with Swiss data protection law and, where applicable, Article 6(1) of the EU GDPR:

a) Processing based on the consent of the data subject.  
b) Processing necessary for the performance of a contract or pre-contractual measures.  
c) Processing required to fulfill a legal obligation.  
d) Processing necessary to protect vital interests of the data subject or another person.  
e) Processing necessary for legitimate interests, including information security, legal compliance, and the enforcement of claims, provided these interests do not override the fundamental rights and freedoms of the data subject.

Personal data is retained only for as long as required for the intended purpose or legal obligations.
